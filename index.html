The Celsius scale was proposed in 1742 by Swedish astronomer Anders Celsius. The Fahrenheit scale was developed by Daniel Gabriel Fahrenheit in 1724, while Lord Kelvin proposed the absolute temperature scale in 1848.

Scientists primarily use Celsius and Kelvin in their measurements. Kelvin is the SI unit of temperature and is used in thermodynamics as it starts from absolute zero, the theoretical point where particles have no thermal energy.
